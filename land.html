Land

Somewhere in the middle of Nebraska, I had an epiphany. I had been gazing up at the spacious skies, imagining the European Pioneers headed west, then the lives of the Native Sioux Tribe, but I arrived at a more humbling point: people had not always been here. I'm certain I had learned this in elementary school, and maybe it was the heat, or a giant grasshopper, but it hit me like a wall. We were not only traversing America, we were traversing a continent. Ages of natural force had shaped this land, and as we segwayed into the American West, this became easier to comprehend. At this moment, I knew America, The Beautiful (Ray Charles’ playing in my head) in a way I never had before.
